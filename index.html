Structure of the System

The system is organized into six interconnected tracks:

📘 X.1 — Formal Learning Track

A structured, mathematically grounded academic spine. Notes, problem sets, derivations, and long-form technical write-ups live here.

🧠 X.2 — Conceptual Learning Track

Extraction of first principles about learning itself: abstraction, generalization, compression, representation, iteration. This is where meta-learning theory is distilled into research artifacts.

🤖 X.3 — Agent Learning Track

An evolving baby agent codebase that ingests inputs, runs experiments, and produces measurable outputs. This is where theory meets implementation.

🧪 X.4 — Agent Learning Research Paper Track

Formal research papers derived from X.3 experiments. These are published artifacts — versioned, citable, and public.

📈 X.5 — Agent Trading Track

An applied extension exploring decision systems in financial environments, emphasizing disciplined experimentation and engineering rigor.

🔁 X.6 — Recursive Self-Improvement Track

The integrator. This track consumes outputs from X.1 through X.5 and proposes improvements to the learning agents and trading agents themselves.

This is the attempt to close the loop 🔄

A grounded exploration of Recursive Self-Improvement (RSI) as an engineering discipline — disciplined, reviewable, pull-request–based iteration.

Autonomous Iteration

Over time, autonomous agents will:

  • 🛠️ propose code changes
  • 🔀 submit pull requests
  • 🏗️ suggest architectural improvements
  • 🧭 refine learning paradigms

with human review acting as the alignment and safety layer.

As knowledge accumulates across disciplines 📚, the agents gain access to a richer conceptual substrate.

As the agents improve, they refine how that knowledge is structured and integrated. The feedback loop strengthens 🔁

If successful, the system approaches a state where the rate of learning itself improves over time ⬆️ — constrained primarily by compute, context limits, and disciplined execution.

🧬 Project BIO - AI-assisted Biological Optimization

In parallel with engineering recursive AI systems, I began applying similar principles of structured feedback, measurement, and iteration to my own biological system - my body 😀

The result was an AI-driven Food & Nutrition Optimization framework — initially targeting the classical 32 essential nutrients, and later expanded to a 40-nutrient model incorporating modern longevity-related factors (Omega-3 balance, polyphenols, lutein, choline, CoQ10, etc.)

Phase 1 — Elimination of Deficiencies

  • 📊 Weekly tracking of 32 essential nutrients against RWA targets
  • 🔁 Iterative adjustment of food inputs based on measurable gaps
  • 💧 Structured hydration targets (3–4L/day)
  • ☀️ Scheduled sunlight exposure for endogenous Vitamin D optimization

Within 12 weeks, chronic recurring issues (frequent infections, gastrointestinal instability, breathing-related sensitivity) that had persisted for me through 2024 & 2025 were completely eliminated through systematic correction of micronutrient, hydration, and sunlight deficiencies.

I have stayed completely infection-free and disease-free from 2025 end through to 2026, even when I take breaks from following this system and cheat with junk food or skip water/sunlight for a few days - my body has good reserves of these nutrients now and has the resilience to absorb many bad days. The sense of accomplishment I feel with that is something I can't even put into words - this is currently one of the biggest examples of how AI has made a genuine difference to my life.

Phase 2 — Expansion to RWA40

The model was extended beyond conventional RDAs to include additional longevity-linked compounds from post-1980s nutritional reseaech:

  • 🧠 Omega-3 ALA / EPA / DHA balance
  • 🌿 Polyphenols & flavonoids
  • 👁️ Lutein + Zeaxanthin
  • ⚡ Choline
  • 🔋 CoQ10

The system now consistently achieves 100%+ weekly coverage across nearly all tracked nutrients through deterministic tracking, AI-assisted planning, and disciplined execution.
